Regional Economic Intelligence Councils (REICs)

The Regional Economic Intelligence Councils (REICs), were first constituted in the year 1996 with formation of 18 REICs. REICs coordinate work amongst intelligence and enforcement agencies of Central and State governments dealing with economic offences at the regional levels. The number of REICs presently stands at 30. REIC is the most important forum at the regional level overseeing Government Agencies responsible for economic intelligence and combating economic offences with the objective to coordinate and strengthen intelligence gathering activities, investigative efforts and enforcement action of various agencies The REICs provide a platform to law enforcement agencies to discuss and address issues related to economic offences at local level. The CEIB monitors and benchmarks the performance of these REICs through regular reviews and ensure that REIC meetings are held with prescribed periodicity.
